Through ADHD Eyes

Mapping the Adult ADHD Healthcare Journey in the Netherlands

Photovoice Participatory Action Research

Study Introduction

Adults with ADHD often face long waits, sensory-busy clinics, and confusing paperwork in Dutch healthcare. Through ADHD Eyes invites 5–6 adults to photograph these moments, pinpoint both barriers and bright spots, and design small “Journey Boosters” that clinics can adopt quickly. Run with the Neurodiversity Foundation, this participatory action research project positions every participant as a co-researcher shaping real change in just eight weeks.

Study Aim

  • Document real-world barriers and facilitators across help-seeking, waiting, and consultation stages.
  • Co-create and pilot low-cost Journey Boosters for ADHD-friendly clinics.
  • Produce a Neuro-Inclusive Care Pledge informed by lived experience.

Method in 3 Steps

  1. Photovoice image capture — each participant takes six photos of helpful or stressful moments.
  2. Reflection & group workshop — together we analyse themes and invent booster ideas.
  3. Booster testing & pledge — participants trial fixes, then co-sign a Neuro-Inclusive Care Pledge.
